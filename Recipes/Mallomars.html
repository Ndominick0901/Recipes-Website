Mallomar Cookies

Description

This Mallomar recipe was used as part of an after dinner cookie plate for patrons to enjoy with their coffee as their main desserts were being made. It's fun to make, because who doesn't love making their own gooey marshmallow from scratch?!

Ingredients

Cookie:

  • (3) Cups All Purpose Flour
  • (1/2) Cup Sugar
  • (1/2) Teaspoon Salt
  • (3/4) Teaspoon Baking Powder
  • (3/8) Teaspoon Baking Soda
  • (1/2) Teaspoon Cinnamon
  • (12) Tablespoons Butter
  • (3) Eggs

Chocolate:

  • Pate Glace

Marshmallow:

  • (1/4) Cup of Water
  • (1/4) Cup of Corn Syrup
  • (3/4) Cup of Sugar
  • (1) Tablespoon of Gelatin Powder
  • (3) Tablespoons of Water
  • (1/4) Teaspoon of Vanilla Extract
  • (2) Egg Whites

Directions

Cookie:

  1. Set oven to 350 degrees
  2. Measure Butter in advance and leave at room temperature for an hour or two to soften
  3. Sift Flour, Sugar, Salt, Baking Powder, Baking Soda, and Cinnamon through a fine mesh sieve
  4. Add dry ingredients to a food processor and gradually add the Butter on pulse setting
  5. Once Butter is mixed with the dry ingredients add egg one-by-one and continue to pulse until a dough is well formed
  6. Remove from processor and form into a cohesive ball. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate
  7. Once dough is slightly chilled remove from the refrigerator and place on countertop to roll out. From a thin sheet with a rolling pin and begin to cut out cookies with a small round cookie cutter. Place the cut outs on a lined baking tray.
  8. Cookies will bake on lower rack for 9-11 Minutes. The edges of the cookie should be golden brown and you will know they are finished.

Chocolate

  • Bring a pot of water to boil. Place Pate Glace over water bath and melt slowly

Marshmallow

  1. In a small bowl mix (3) Tablespoons H2O with (1/4) Teaspoon of Vanilla Extract.
  2. Sprinkle Gelatin Powder evenly over the Water and Extract mixture to bloom
  3. Bring (1/4) Cup of H2O, Corn Syrup, and Sugar to a boil in a small stainless steel saucepan
  4. When the Water and sugar mixture is boiling, remove from the heat and add bloomed gelatin. Mix quickly until well combined
  5. Whip Egg Whites in a stand up mixer on high speed until forming soft peaks. While mixer is still on high, add the Water, Sugar, Gelatin mixture to the Egg Whites in a very slow stream. Focus on adding the stream to the center of the mixer rather than the sides
  6. Marshmallow will begin to form and should be quite stiff but still very pliable
  7. After marshmallow is formed add to a piping bag, cut a small slit in the bottom of the piping bag and pipe Marshmallow onto cooled cookies
  8. Once all of the cookies have Marshmallow topping and the marshmallow is set, spoon the melted Pate Glace over to coat
  9. Remove cookies from the baking sheet with an offset spatula and place on a wire rack
