Creamy Caesar Dressing

Description

This Ceasar Dressing has been iterated upon for many years and can will adapt to be enjoyed year round. It is extremely flexible, and as long as you keep the base recipe the same it can be updated to fit whatever season you are experiencing. For instance, in the summer I will add fresh basil, reduce the red wine vinegar, and make up the liquid with fresh lemon juice for a more refreshing dressing!

Ingredients

  • (8) Egg Yolks
  • (2) Whole Eggs
  • (1 1/4) Cup Dijon Mustard
  • (6) Ounces Anchovy Paste
  • (1/2) Tablespoon of White Pepper
  • (1/2) Cup of Garlic Puree
  • (1/2) Cup of Lemon Juice
  • (1) Cup of Red Wine Vinegar
  • (1/4) Cup of Worcestshire Sauce
  • (4) Teaspoons of Salt
  • (6) Cups of Extra Light Olive Oil

Instructions

  1. Blend Garlic in food processor with a small amount of oil until it forms a puree
  2. Transfer Garlic Puree to standing blender and Add Egg Yolks, Whole Eggs, Dijon Mustard, Anchovy Paste, White Pepper, Lemon Juice, Red Wine Vinegar, Worcestshire Sauce, and Salt
  3. Blend the ingredients on medium/low for approximately 45 seconds or until there is a fine puree
  4. Set blender to low and while processing slowly add Extra Light Olive Oil in a slow stream
  5. Mixture will begin to emulsify as Oil is slowly added (it may not be necessary to add the full amount of oil, so you can stop adding oil and shut off blender when emulsification reaches desired creaminess)
